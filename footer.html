-----------------------------The Future of Coding In Primary Eductation-----------------------------

Annotated Bibliography:

Solochek, Jeffrey S. "CODING SKILLS GAIN VALUE; A bill would let students choose computers over a foreign language." Tampa Bay Times [St. Petersburg, FL], 19 Oct. 2015, p. 1A. Gale In Context: Opposing Viewpoints, link.gale.com/apps/doc/A431979663/OVIC?u=cps1440&sid=bookmark-OVIC&xid=f4064f0d. The article "Coding Skills Gain Value" by Jeffrey S. Solochek refers to a legislative proposal in Florida in 2015, that would have allowed high school students to substitute computer coding classes for foreign language requirements. Proponents argue that the skill of coding is one necessary for students entering the workforce of today; critics point to the loss of both cultural and linguistic learning in these substitutions. The discussion here reflects the increased emphasis on computer literacy in education and its future potential in creating career paths. This is symptomatic of much wider trends within educational policy, which emphasize technology and STEM fields. The source, "Coding Skills Gain Value" by Jeffrey S. Solochek, helps to look at debates over education policy, focus on a particular Florida proposal, and explores the pros and cons of computer coding over traditional foreign languages. The source is credible, having been published in a well-respected newspaper, the Tampa Bay Times, and made available through Gale's, which assures its reliability.

Delacruz, Stacy. “Starting From Scratch (Jr.): Integrating Code Literacy in the Primary Grades.” The Reading Teacher, vol. 73, no. 6, 2020, pp. 805–11. JSTOR, https://www.jstor.org/stable/27003618.The source is on the benefits of teaching coding in primary schools: how it improves problem-solving skills, logical thinking, and creativity among young students. It describes how early exposure to programming helps build digital literacy. Which has become very important in today's world. The article also highlights some of the problems in implementing coding curriculums, such as resource constraints and teacher training. This academic source is helpful for educators, policymakers, and researchers willing to examine the effectiveness of coding education. The article "Starting from Scratch (Jr.): Integrating Code Literacy in the Primary Grades" by Stacy Delacruz, published in The Reading Teacher journal, is a credible source. It explains how primary school students can use Scratch Jr. to learn coding in fun.

“Cracking the Code | Chicago Public Schools.” Chicago Public Schools, 3 Mar. 2020, https://www.cps.edu/sites/blog/search/2020/march/cracking-the-code/. The blog post "Cracking the Code" by Chicago Public Schools talks about teaching students how to code and use computer science skills. It explains programs that help students become better problem-solvers and prepare for jobs in technology. The post also focuses on making sure all students have the chance to learn coding, no matter where they come from. It shares stories from students and teachers who have benefited from these programs and shows how coding is being added to schools in the district. The source “Cracking the Code | Chicago Public Schools” is reliable because it comes from Chicago Public Schools (CPS). Since CPS is a trusted source, the information is likely accurate, but it might be a little biased because they focus more on positive things about their programs and leave out any negatives to make them look better. The blog post was published on March 3, 2020, which means the information might not be up-to-date, especially if it’s about technology or programs that could have changed since then. The purpose of the article is to share information about CPS initiatives and to promote their work, so it might not include a lot of critical analysis or opposing viewpoints.

K, Jamie. “Teachers’ Essential Guide to Coding in the Classroom | Common Sense Education.” Common Sense Education, 15 Nov. 2024, https://www.commonsense.org/education/articles/teachers-essential-guide-to-coding-in-the-classroom. The article "Teachers' Essential Guide to Coding in the Classroom" by Common Sense Education gives teachers a starting point and a path to follow as they work to incorporate coding into their practice. It helps to emphasize the importance of coding as one of the most fundamental skills because of how the modern jobs are trending to needing skills in computer science. The guide also provides recommendations for coding platforms, tips on starting a coding curriculum, and suggestions for overcoming challenges when teaching programming concepts. This resource is ideal for any teacher new to coding or looking to improve current classroom practices with technology. This is a credible and reliable source. This is a trusted source because it is a nonprofit organization known for its promotion of digital literacy and safe technology use in education. Information is accurate and updated with practical guidance based on current educational trends and tools such as Scratch and Code.org, which are well recognized. This should be of high relevance to K-12 educators, as it explains why coding is a basic skill and then gives actionable strategies and tools to help teachers integrate this into their classrooms.

"Arkansas' School-Coding Initiative Centers on Teacher PD; Arkansas isn't the first jurisdiction to set aggressive coding-education objectives, but its emphasis on teacher training has set it apart." Education Week, vol. 35, no. 35, 9 June 2016, p. 25. Gale In Context: Opposing Viewpoints, link.gale.com/apps/doc/A455788733/OVIC?u=cps1440&sid=bookmark-OVIC&xid=b3bfbce2 This source discusses how Arkansas' state-led initiative brought computer science education to every high school, while funding teacher training and professional development to ensure the quality of instruction. The result is a huge increase in the number of students enrolling in computer science courses and teachers being certified in the field. This source is reliable because it points to a very specific, documented initiative that was supported by state policies and funding. The source aims to illustrate how robust teacher training can help introduce computer science into public schools. The information is well-structured, though it does not include detailed long-term outcomes or challenges faced.

"Coding to be mandatory for younger students." Sydney Morning Herald [Sydney, Australia], 21 Aug. 2018, p. 17. Gale In Context: Opposing Viewpoints, link.gale.com/apps/doc/A550842703/OVIC?u=cps1440&sid=bookmark-OVIC&xid=8eb7dbac. Accessed 16 Dec. 2024. The article talks of a mandate to include teaching coding in the science and technology curriculum for primary school going students. The program makes sure that by the high school level, every school student is equipped with fundamentals in coding and computational thinking, and that coding has gained a place in general education, accessible to all irrespective of their background. This is a valid source; it reports on the educational policy and its stated aim. It shows that learning the skills of coding should begin earlier in life if a country wants to raise competent citizens for the future labor markets. However, it doesn't tackle the specific difficulties, like funding or shortages, which schools may undergo trying to implement the mandate.

Moxley, Tania. “Coding for the Careers of the Future.” Virginia Tech, Virginia Tech, 1 Mar. 2024, https://news.vt.edu/articles/2024/02/coe-cs-coding-careers-of-the-future.html. This source gives some information on the outreach by VirginiaTech to develop kids interest in coding or computer science usingCodeKids. The program covers activities like building open-source games, interactive, and fitting with state educational standards. Stressing collaboration between university students and local schools for changing the way coding is currently taught. This is a credible source because it involves one of the finest universities, and it reveals a very innovative program to advance education in the STEM areas. That demonstrates how partnerships between universities and public schools can provide accessible tools for teaching coding. However, this source focuses on the pilot phase and has not represented data on its broadening impact. “Coding the Future: How Programming Is Shaping the Next Decade – Ledger.” Yale University – WordPress for Individuals and Groups @ Yale University, The Yale Ledger, 2023, https://campuspress.yale.edu/ledger/coding-the-future-how-programming-is-shaping-the-next-decade/. This article explores in detail how coding has developed into a core skill that will shape the business, education, and environmental sectors. Further, it has elaborated on the role of coding in enhancing productivity, collaboration, and innovation within industries. Additionally, its growing importance in climate change mitigation and universal language in the modern digital era is highlighted. This is a credible source because it talks of the pervasiveness with which coding has come into the lives of people from all walks of life: education, healthcare, and environmental sustainability. It's An informative article that gives many examples from these three fields. Its focus is broad, with an emphasis on the importance of coding, but it lacks the specific challenges of integrating coding into schools.
